Airline Application

Overview

Problem

This is proof of concept of an airline application for a South American airline. Under NDA all branding elements and information that can identify the client have been replaced. The airline's services were primarily accessed through their website. Although they had a mobile application, it was not a native application and rendered the original website in a mobile format. This approach had really bad user experience as none of the mobile interactions (swipe, double tap) were utilized neither were the interactive elements designed to adjust to small mobile screens. The objective of this initiative was to provide a native application that provided the best user experience for mobile users

My Role

Interaction Designer - Involved in creating journey map and lo-fi prototype. Lead designer for high fidelity prototype
Low-fi prototype was created in proto.io while hi-fi was created using Adobe creative suite with invision for interaction. Dev handoff was done in Zeplin.

Research

Journey Map

As part of NIIT Tech's offering they have consulted and designed for multiple European and American Airlines. I sat down with subject matter experts, consultants working in the airline industry to gain insight into the industry. Based on the user data provided by the consultants a journey map was created for a typical vacationer.

Design

Flight Search

The flight search page was redesigned to provide a better user experience on a mobile screen
1. All screens in the application including the flight search screen have only 1 primary call-to-action unlike the multiple call-to-actions in the previous design
2. Hamburger menu was replaced with a tab styled menu so that none of the functions are hidden from the user
3. The flight search screen was decluttered to show the most important information to user

Ancillary services

Ancillary services offer passengers services other that flight booking or items covered under your ticket. These services include in-flight entertainment, meals during the journey, booking cabs or hotel rooms after you have reached the destination.

Apart from being an additional source of revenue for the airlines, these services create strong loyalty among the customers when linked with flyer miles.

It also improves the customer experience by not only meeting their in-flight needs like buying meals and shopping but also taking care of their post-travel needs by allowing passengers to book cabs and hotels while they are traveling.

Mobile receipts

Based on the market analysis of the South American airline, their user's used a variety of payment methods with direct bank transactions being the most used method. To aid direct bank transactions we created mobile receipts which can be shown to the bank teller to complete the transaction.

Notifications and chatbot

Integration of a chatbot to take care of common pre-flight tasks can relieve user anxiety. Users are checked in before the flight and they get their mobile boarding passes which can be accessed through the application. Users also get a reminder from the application to carry their documents like passport and visa. Users can ask basic queries about weather conditions at the destination or their flight status.

Document Wizard

The country in which the airline operates is seen as a vacation destination so they get a lot of travelers from other countries. Most of these travelers would require valid visa and passport. The wizard determines these requirements based on the user's nationality. This improves the user's experience in planning a trip and getting the right set of documents to visit the country

Designing for different markets

South American markets work differently than American markets. These differences challenged me to come up with design solutions that managed to integrate mobiles in an infrastructure that still used physical artefacts in financial transactions. This shows the value of user research and how good research can create solutions that are innovative but not necessarily disruptive
